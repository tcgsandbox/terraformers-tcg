# The First Shaping

Before the rivers learned to run, before the forests learned to drink the rain, there was only the Worldspring — a single point of bright water, suspended in the dark.

The gods were many then, and most of them were patient. They watched the spring for a long time without touching it. They argued about what it might become. Some said it should be a sea. Some said it should be a stone. Some said it should be left alone.

Then one god, whose name has been deliberately forgotten, reached down and pulled the spring open like a wound. Water spilled into the void in every direction. Where it landed, it became something — a slope, a shore, a tangle of green.

The other gods, startled, reached down too. They scraped at the world with their hands. They piled up mountains. They sank the oceans. They breathed life into the warm places and ice into the cold ones. The world was shaped not in a single moment, but in a long, jealous quarrel.

When the quarrel finally paused, none of the gods could agree on what they had made. So they did not leave. They sat upon their hills and their seas and their burning sands and they waited, each one watching the others, each one ready to reach down again and reshape what offended them.

The waiting has not ended.
